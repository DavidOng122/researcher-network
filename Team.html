General Surgeon

Dr. Hardy completed her Doctor of Medicine (2002) at the University of Toronto and her General Surgery Residency (2008) at the University of Manitoba followed by a Fellowship in Minimally Invasive Surgery at the University of Toronto (2010). Her research training included a Master of Science in Surgery degree at the University of Manitoba during surgical residency. She is a Fellow of the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada as well as the American College of Surgeons.

Dr. Hardy is an Associate Professor at the University of Manitoba with a clinical practice of Minimally Invasive Surgery at St. Boniface General Hospital and Victoria General Hospital. She currently serves as Graduate Chair for the Department of Surgery and is an active member of the Clinician Investigator Program Committee, Research Advisory Committee, Surgery Education Advisory Committee and General Surgery Competency Committee. Dr. Hardy is also the research lead at the Centre for Metabolic and Bariatric Surgery. Dr. Ashley Vergis

mail

General Surgeon

Dr. Vergis completed his Doctor of Medicine (2002) and residency in general surgery (2008) at the University of Manitoba while concurrently earning a Masters of Medical Education through the University of Dundee, Scotland. He then attained a clinical fellowship in Bariatric and Minimally Invasive Surgery at the University of Toronto after obtaining his Canadian and American Board Certifications in surgery.

He is currently a Professor of surgery at the University of Manitoba and is an active member of the minimally invasive surgery program at St. Boniface and Victoria General Hospital. Dr. Vergis holds several clinical leadership positions including clinical lead for the provincial Bariatric Surgery Program and the director of Surgical Peritoneal Dialysis for the Manitoba Renal Program. His educational leadership positions include post graduate surgical skills director, general surgery residency endoscopy director, and the program director for the minimally invasive and bariatric surgery fellowship at the University of Manitoba.

Dr. Wenjing He

mail

Research Associate

Dr. He brings a rich background in medicine, merging extensive surgical expertise with a profound commitment to advancing healthcare education. Her journey commenced with rigorous medical training at Xinxiang Medical University and Shantou Medical University, where she honed her skills as a surgeon. The pursuit of excellence led her to Canada in 2014, marking a pivotal moment in her career.

A testament to her dedication, Dr. He completed her Ph.D. training at the University of Alberta in 2019, solidifying her position as a thought leader in the field. Currently serving as a Research Associate in the Department of Surgery at the University of Manitoba, she actively engages with a collaborative network that spans surgeons, engineers, clinical educators, and psychologists.

Dr. He's research is at the forefront of innovation, focusing on the development of simulators and the evaluation of educational outcomes within simulation-based programs. Her long-term vision is leveraging simulation in surgery to enhance care quality and elevate patient safety standards.
