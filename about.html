About Us

Our General Surgery Research Team, situated at St Boniface General Hospital in Winnipeg Manitoba, is at the forefront of exploring new avenues in clinical trials and surgical simulation. We strive to make meaningful contributions to the field of surgery with our focus on patient care and surgical proficiency.

Our Research Focus:

  1. Clinical Trials:

    Our team actively engages in clinical trials to assess and implement the latest advancements in surgical procedures, technologies, and treatment modalities, aiming to introduce evidence-based practices that elevate the standard of surgical care and ensure optimal patient outcomes. Our research spans diverse themes, including perioperative optimization of bariatric outcomes, predictors of bariatric surgery outcomes, Indigenous bariatric surgery, and acute care surgery outcomes. We explore areas such as fitness and nutritional outcomes, geography/access to care, family physician experience, sleep apnea, and virtual care in the context of bariatric surgery. Our commitment extends to predicting factors like length of stay, bariatric biomarker profiles, and considering the influence of geography and Indigenous status. Additionally, we conduct foundational work to understand the Indigenous experience with bariatric surgery, aiming to incorporate Indigenous approaches to wellness in bariatric care. Our research themes align with interests in bariatric surgery, minimally invasive surgery, and acute care surgery, all contributing to advancements in surgical knowledge and patient care.

  2. Surgical Simulation:

    Our team places a strong emphasis on Surgical Simulation, acknowledging the vital role of hands-on training in surgical proficiency. Devoted to the development and refinement of surgical simulation programs, we offer a risk-free environment for surgeons to augment their skills, explore new techniques, and enhance patient safety. In our commitment to innovation, our Surgical Simulation programs integrate cutting-edge technologies such as eye tracking, facial expressions tracking, 3D printing, and more. These advanced tools contribute to the realism and effectiveness of our simulations, offering a comprehensive training experience that aligns with the latest technological advancements in the field of surgery.
